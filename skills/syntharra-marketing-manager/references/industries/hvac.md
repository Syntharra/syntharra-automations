# HVAC Industry Profile — Marketing

## Business Profile

**Who they are:** HVAC contractors, 1–20 employees, owner-operated.
Owner often on the tools all day — can't answer every call.
High ticket jobs ($500 repair → $15,000 system install).
Heavily seasonal (peaks: summer + winter). Competition is fierce.

**Biggest pain points:**
1. Missing calls while on a job or after hours
2. Losing leads to competitors who answer faster
3. Emergency calls at night/weekends — nobody to answer
4. Spending too much time on the phone instead of working

**Decision makers:** Owner/operator (Mike, 40–60yo, not very tech-savvy, trusts outcomes not features)

## Hook Angles That Work

These frames resonate with HVAC business owners:

| Angle | Hook |
|---|---|
| After-hours emergency | "Your phone rang at 9pm Friday. Did you answer?" |
| Lost revenue | "What does 1 missed $8,000 AC replacement cost you?" |
| Competitor advantage | "While you were on the roof, your competitor took your lead." |
| Simple stat | "37% of after-hours HVAC calls go to voicemail. Most don't call back." |
| Seasonal urgency | "July is your busiest month. It's also when you miss the most calls." |

## Seasonal Timing for HVAC Outreach

**Best time to run HVAC campaigns:**
- April–May: Pre-summer push (before the chaos starts)
- September–October: Pre-winter push (heat systems)
- January: Post-holiday, planning mode

**Avoid:** June–August (they're slammed, don't have time to talk)
**Avoid:** December 20 – January 5 (holiday slowdown)
