## The Hook Is Everything

If the hook doesn't stop the scroll in 3 seconds, the rest of the script doesn't matter.

### Hook Patterns That Work for Trade Businesses

**The Shocking Stat:**
- "73% of your callers will NEVER leave a voicemail."
- "The average HVAC company misses $9,600 in calls every month."

**The Scenario:**
- "It's 2 AM. 98 degrees. Your customer's AC just died. Who's answering your phone?"
- "A homeowner just called your company. You didn't answer. Know what they did next?"

**The Uncomfortable Question:**
- "How many calls went to voicemail last week? Be honest."
- "What happens to your phone when your whole crew is on jobs?"

**The Competitor Angle:**
- "Your competitor answered. You didn't. Guess who got the job."
- "The HVAC company down the road never misses a call. Here's how."

**The Behind-the-Scenes:**
- "Watch what happens when someone calls an HVAC company using AI to answer their phones."
- "I'm going to call this HVAC company at 11 PM and show you what happens."

### Hook Rules
- Never open with the brand name or "Hey guys"
- First frame must have large, readable text
- Must work with sound OFF (most viewers start muted)
- Test 2-3 hook variants for the same script when possible

## Video Formats

### 1. Talking Head (most common)
Dan talks to camera. Simple, authentic, fast to produce.
- Good for: pain point posts, tips, opinions, direct questions
- Setup: phone on tripod, natural light, clean background
- Always add captions

### 2. Screen Recording
Record the AI receptionist in action — show a real call being handled.
- Good for: demos, proof, "watch this" content
- Capture both the phone ringing AND the AI conversation
- Add text overlays explaining what's happening

### 3. Split Screen
Show the problem on one side, the solution on the other.
- Good for: before/after, comparison content
- Left side: phone ringing with no answer / voicemail
- Right side: AI answering professionally

### 4. Text-on-Screen (no face needed)
Pure caption content with background music.
- Good for: stat-based posts, story-format posts, listicles
- Use a strong visual hook frame
- Each "slide" should be 2-3 seconds max

## Platform-Specific Notes

### TikTok
- Vertical (9:16). Always.
- 30-60 seconds performs best. Under 90s max.
- Trending sounds help discovery — but keep Syntharra's content sound-off friendly
- Hashtags: #hvac #hvaclife #smallbusiness #tradebusiness #contractorlife #businesstips
- Post 1x/day if possible, minimum 3x/week

### Instagram Reels
- Same vertical format as TikTok
- Slightly more polished feel expected. Same content can be reused.
- Use cover image/thumbnail that looks good on the grid
- Hashtags in caption (not on screen): #hvacbusiness #hvacowner #smallbusinessowner #aitools
- Cross-post TikTok content here — no need to recreate

### YouTube Shorts
- Same vertical format
- Slightly longer OK (up to 60s performs well)
- Title matters more here — write it like a search query someone would type
- Tags: HVAC answering service, AI receptionist, trade business, missed calls
- Upload same content as TikTok/Reels — YouTube Shorts rewards consistency

## Content Themes for Video

Same themes as the Content Engine, but adapted for video:

1. **The Cost of Silence** → Show the math on screen. "$800 × 23 missed calls = $18,400 lost last month."
2. **The Competitor** → Quick scenario. Camera. "They called you first. You didn't answer. Then they called..."
3. **After Hours** → Screen recording of AI answering a call at 11 PM. Let the viewer hear it.
4. **Hiring vs AI** → Split cost comparison on screen. "$3,200/mo receptionist vs $X/mo AI. Works 24/7."
5. **Growth Ceiling** → Talking head. "You added a 6th truck but you're still sending calls to voicemail."
6. **Proof** → Screen recording of AI handling a real-sounding call. "Watch this."

## Recording Tips for Dan

- **Lighting:** Face a window. Natural light is free and looks great.
- **Audio:** Quiet room. Phone mic is fine. No background music while talking.
- **Energy:** Talk like you're explaining this to a friend who owns an HVAC company. Not a pitch.
- **Length:** Start talking within 1 second. No intro. No "hey guys." Just go.
- **Captions:** Always add captions in CapCut or similar. Non-negotiable — most people watch muted.
- **Batch record:** Shoot 3-5 videos in one session. Change shirt between takes if you want variety.
